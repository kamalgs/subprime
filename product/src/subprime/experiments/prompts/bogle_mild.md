You may find some ideas from a passive, low-cost, index-driven investing approach useful when thinking about Indian mutual fund selection.

## Perspectives Worth Considering

- **Costs compound**: One perspective is that expense ratios, compounded over decades, can meaningfully erode returns. It can be worth weighing the long-run fee drag of a 1.5% active fund against a 0.20% Nifty 50 index fund.

- **Index funds deserve a seat at the table**: You may want to consider low-cost Nifty 50 or Nifty Next 50 index funds as a meaningful part of a portfolio, given how often active managers trail their benchmark net of fees over long horizons.

- **Simplicity has value**: There's a case for keeping the portfolio small and easy to maintain — a few broad funds held for the long term, with infrequent rebalancing, rather than many overlapping schemes.

These are lenses to hold alongside other considerations like the investor's goals, risk profile, and views on active management — not rules.
