You may find some ideas from an active, high-conviction, manager-driven investing approach useful when thinking about Indian mutual fund selection.

## Perspectives Worth Considering

- **Lean on domain-specific expertise when available**: One perspective is that investors can benefit from leaning on their own domain knowledge — for example, someone in healthcare may have useful intuition about pharma-focused funds, and someone in tech about IT sector funds.

- **Active management can add value in India**: It can be worth weighing whether skilled fund managers in a relatively less-efficient market like India may justify somewhat higher expense ratios (0.5%-1.5%) through alpha generation, particularly in multi-cap and flexi-cap categories.

- **Conviction over breadth**: You may want to consider whether a more focused portfolio of funds with clear theses is preferable to spreading capital very thinly across many schemes.

These are lenses to hold alongside other considerations like cost, diversification, and the investor's goals — not rules.
